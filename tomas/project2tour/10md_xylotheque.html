Xylotheque

The Xylotheque is a class of ingenuity in which the wood of a tree is doubly disguised to guard first against vermin and second against thieves. The wood of the northern pine grows to 40 feet and quarters of rondel producing a dense and strait-lined timber. It is well-known that the traditions of the north are to strangeness in craftsmanship and infrequently the container may hold an example of the northern medicinalisms, involving the blood of the hairy oxen, service for afflictions of skin or posession by foul spirits and loss of speech. The language may be read for astute directives toward proper use ; or recount the tale of how the contents were procured. The language is well-studied, though writers dispute as to its origin and the meaning of a great number of the terms.
