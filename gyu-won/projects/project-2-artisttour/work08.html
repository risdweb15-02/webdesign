9a Bienal do Mercosul identity system

December 2012

Sofía Hernández Chong Cuy, Chief Curator of the 9a Bienal do Mercosul in Porto Alegre, Brazil, commissioned Project Projects to develop a comprehensive identity system that would itself reflect upon historical and contemporary technologies of communication. At the core of the new system is a display typeface that acts as an archive of found symbols, and enacts translation within every glyph.

Custom-designed by Project Projects for the Bienal, the bespoke typeface Porto Alegre draws upon symbols found in several scientific notational systems: from meteorological charts and condition maps, to vintage prototypical versions of the Periodic Table that pre-date its current typographic system. In the months leading up to the Bienal, a fluid spectrum of color values evoke, forecast, and trace the oxidizing tones of brown copper minerals and atmospheric shades of blue.
