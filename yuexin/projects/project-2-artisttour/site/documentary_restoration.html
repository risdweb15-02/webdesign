Simon Denny performs a restoration on a documentary that focuses on a speculation over the possible connection between the chameleon-like disease Chronic Fatigue Syndrome or Myalgic Encephalomyelitis, a largely unexplained medical condition characterized by pervasive exhaustion and muscle aches, recognized in the mid 1980s, that effects the lives of roughly 17 Million people worldwide, and the newly discovered retrovirus XMRV which has striking structural similarities to HIV.

The exhibition will focus on a number of ‘split-screen prints’ – demonstrations showing dramatic improvements in picture quality – before and after the restoration process.
